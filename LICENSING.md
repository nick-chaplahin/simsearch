# SimSearch Licensing Guide

SimSearch uses a **dual licensing model** — the same software is
available under two different licenses depending on how you use it.
This model is used by well-known projects such as MySQL, Qt, and MongoDB.

## What Does the GPL Require?

If you use the GPL license, you must:

- ✅ Make your source code available to users under the GPL
- ✅ Include the LICENSE-GPL.txt file in your distribution
- ✅ Clearly state any modifications you've made
- ✅ Not add additional restrictions beyond what the GPL allows

You **cannot**:
- ❌ Keep your source code proprietary
- ❌ Use the software in a closed-source commercial product
- ❌ Remove copyright notices

---

## What Does the Commercial License Allow?

If you purchase a Commercial License, you can:

- ✅ Keep your source code private and proprietary
- ✅ Embed SimSearch in a closed-source product
- ✅ Distribute binaries without releasing source
- ✅ Provide the software as part of a SaaS product
- ✅ Get priority support and updates

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I'm a startup with $4,800K revenue — do I need a commercial license?**
No. You are under the $1M threshold and can use the software under the GPL,
provided you comply with the GPL terms (open source requirement).

**Q: My company earns $5M/year but I'm only using this internally. Do I need a license?**
Yes. Revenue threshold applies regardless of how you use the software.
You would need the Startup Commercial License.

**Q: I want to use SimSearch in a closed-source tool but my company is small.**
The GPL requires you to open-source your code. If you don't want to do that,
you need a Commercial License regardless of your revenue.

**Q: We're building an open source project — can we use SimSearch?**
Yes! As long as your project is licensed under a GPL-compatible license
(GPL v2/v3, AGPL, LGPL, etc.), you can use SimSearch for free.

**Q: Do I need one license per developer or per company?**
Commercial Licenses are per-organization. A single license covers all
developers and deployments within your company.

**Q: What happens if my revenue grows beyond my current tier?**
You should upgrade your license tier at your next renewal. We do not
retroactively charge for mid-year revenue growth.
